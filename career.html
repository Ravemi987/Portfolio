Field Insights

Béatrice

Digital Forensics Laboratory Manager
Directorate General of Armament (DGA)

"The role of the DGA is to develop equipment for our military forces. As a Digital Forensic Manager in cyberdefense, I analyze every device likely to contain information: from phones and hard drives to memory and critical systems. This role demands solid knowledge in systems, networks, software engineering and cybersecurity. It also requires a lot of patience, as you must be meticulous in your research. I am fortunate to work with devices and systems at the cutting edge of technology, and to work on projects that you simply won't see anywhere else."

Jaime

Technical Manager for R&T in Aerial Surveillance
Thales

"As a Technical lead in Research and Technology within the defense sector, my mission is to explore the scientific feasibility of implementing new technologies, especially AI, by creating prototypes and demonstrators. The main goal is to reduce the risks and prepare the development phase. It requires strong skills in artificial intelligence (Computer Vision, LLM, Deep Learning), as well as the ability to program according to RAMS principles (Reliability, Availability, Maintainability, Safety)."

Enzo

Systems Verification & Validation (V&V) Engineer
Airbus Defense & Space

"As a Validation Engineer, my main mission is to design, lead and automate testing processes for electronic cards and equipment embedded in satellites. My work really sits at the intersection of computer science and electronics. At times, I spend several months programming in C, Python or VHDL. Other times, I perform wiring and operate measurement instruments such as oscilloscopes, signal generators and power supplies, interfaced with a computer in order to form a test rack. This simulates the satellite's environment before launch. Systems validation demands extreme rigor, as maintenance is no longer possible once a satellite is in deep space."
